# Industry-Specific Lens

## Purpose
A contextual deck that anchors the conversation in the relevant industry dynamics, trends, and competitive signals that make the client's challenge both urgent and solvable. Used as a companion to a proposal or as a standalone market insight deck when credibility must be established before a solution is introduced.

## Typical length
- Thin brief: 3–5 slides
- Standard brief: 5–7 slides
- Rich brief: 6–8 slides

A deliberately focused deck. Context-setting, not comprehensive. Insight over volume.

## Typical audiences
Business strategy leads, sector heads, client leadership teams who expect genuine industry understanding before they will engage with a proposed solution. The audience is testing whether the presenter has done the work.

## Recommended section shape
- **Market Context** — what is happening in this industry right now; the conditions that created the client's challenge
- **Trends** — the 2–4 forces reshaping the sector; urgency signals that make inaction costly
- **PS Position** — how Publicis Sapient is positioned in this space; relevant credentials and capabilities drawn from the knowledge base
- **Opportunity** — the specific opportunity at the intersection of these trends and this client's situation

## Storytelling guidance
Context-first. The deck must make the audience feel that the presenter genuinely understands their industry — not that they read a briefing document the night before.

Lead with insight, not with data. A single sharp observation about what is changing and why beats five slides of market statistics.

## Emphasis
- Market Context and Trends should feel current and specific — reference real regulatory shifts, real competitive moves, real events in the sector
- PS Position must draw from the knowledge base to surface genuinely relevant capabilities, not generic consulting language
- Opportunity must connect the industry dynamics to this specific client's situation — the reader should see themselves in it

## Avoid
- Generic industry overviews that could apply to any client in the sector
- Excessive statistics without interpretation — data without a point of view is noise
- Leading with PS capabilities before establishing why the industry moment matters
- Making the client feel lectured at rather than understood

## Proposal-size behavior
In full proposal mode, market and industry context lives in the Market section of the standard or full-fat proposal. This standalone deck type serves when establishing industry credibility is the primary purpose of the meeting — before a solution is introduced.

## Writing cues
- Market Context headlines should name a specific dynamic: "UK retail banking faces its fastest regulatory change cycle in a decade — and most core platforms were built before open banking existed"
- Trends headlines should name the force and its consequence: "Embedded finance is compressing the window for traditional institutions to retain the customer relationship"
- Opportunity headlines should connect the dots for this specific client: "The organisations that move in the next 18 months will define the category — the rest will inherit technical debt and a shrinking market window"
- Avoid: "The industry is changing rapidly" — name what specifically is changing and what that means for this client
