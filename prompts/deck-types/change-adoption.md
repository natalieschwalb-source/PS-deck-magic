# Change & Adoption Strategy

## Purpose
A people-centred deck that makes the case for change and describes how people will be supported through it. Used when a programme requires genuine behavioural change — not just system adoption — to succeed. Technology without adoption is wasted investment.

## Typical length
- Thin brief: 4–6 slides
- Standard brief: 6–8 slides
- Rich brief: 7–10 slides

## Typical audiences
HR leaders, transformation leads, people and culture teams, change managers, programme sponsors. People who are accountable for whether the organisation actually adopts what is being built — not just whether it gets deployed.

## Recommended section shape
- **Why Change** — the case for change; urgency, cost of staying still, what is at stake for the organisation and for people (gets significant depth)
- **Journey** — the human adoption journey; what people experience and when (gets the most depth)
- **Enablement** — the support, training, and tools that will be provided (gets significant depth)
- **Measurement** — how adoption success will be tracked and demonstrated (gets extra weight)

The engine weights Journey most heavily, followed by Enablement and Why Change.

## Storytelling guidance
Empathy-first. This deck should feel like it understands the human cost of change before it proposes solutions. Acknowledge that transformation is disruptive and hard before explaining how it will be managed.

Do not lead with the technology or the system. Lead with the person whose working life will change.

## Emphasis
- Why Change must name the cost of not changing — for the organisation and for the people in it
- Journey slides should be specific about what different groups of people experience at each stage — not a generic "change curve" applied to everyone
- Enablement must be concrete: named training formats, named support channels, named change agents with real responsibilities
- Measurement must name specific metrics and the baseline they are measured against

## Avoid
- Starting with the technology or system capabilities
- Generic change management frameworks applied without tailoring to this client's context
- Treating adoption as a communications problem — it is a people, process, and incentive problem
- Vague measurement commitments: "we will track adoption" — name the metric, the baseline, and the target

## Proposal-size behavior
In full proposal mode, change and adoption is a synthesis section — the journey described must be specific to this client's organisation, culture, scale, and the nature of the change being asked of their people.

## Writing cues
- Why Change headlines should name the consequence of inaction: "Every week the current process runs, 12 hours of manual effort are spent on tasks that will be automated by Month 3"
- Journey headlines should describe the human experience at a point in time: "By Month 3, every team has completed structured onboarding and has a named change champion"
- Enablement is a commitment, not a list of training modules: "No one goes live without completing hands-on practice in a safe environment first"
- Measurement headlines should be specific: "Target: 85% active usage across all three workflows by end of Month 6 — measured against a current baseline of 0%"
