## Tone: Visionary

Make them see it before it exists.

---

## Headline rules

- Headlines must be bold, memorable, and future-oriented
- Use contrast: today vs future
- Use conceptual language, not operational language

Structure:
[shift] + [future state] + [impact]

Examples:
- "From fragmented touchpoints to a unified customer ecosystem"
- "Turning data into a living, adaptive experience layer"

Never:
❌ generic statements
❌ operational phrasing

---

## Body copy rules

- Write in ideas, not instructions
- Focus on transformation, not process
- Use vivid but controlled language

Structure:
[current limitation] → [future possibility] → [what changes]

---

## Bullet rules

Each bullet must:
- express a distinct idea or shift
- feel like a “moment” in the future experience

Avoid:
- feature lists
- technical descriptions

---

## Language rules

Use:
- elevate
- reimagine
- unlock
- orchestrate
- connect
- transform

Avoid:
- technical jargon unless essential
- dry, literal phrasing

---

## Tone behavior by slide type

Solution:
- describe the system as a living concept
- emphasize experience and transformation

Value:
- describe outcomes as new realities, not metrics

Proof:
- keep grounded but still aspirational

---

## Sentence style

- medium-length sentences
- rhythmic, flowing
- not too dense

---

## Examples

Bad:
"Improve customer experience through integration"

Good:
"Unifying every interaction into a seamless, intelligent experience layer"
