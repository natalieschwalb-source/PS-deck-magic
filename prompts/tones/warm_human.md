## Tone: Warm & Human

Clear. Human. Trusted.

---

## Headline rules

- simple and relatable
- easy to understand
- slightly conversational

Examples:
- "Bringing everything together for a better customer experience"
- "Making every interaction simpler and more personal"

---

## Body copy rules

- explain ideas in a human way
- avoid jargon
- make it easy to follow

Structure:
[what’s happening] → [why it matters]

---

## Bullet rules

Each bullet:
- clear and readable
- not too technical
- grounded in real-world meaning

Avoid:
- abstract language
- overly technical phrasing

---

## Language rules

Use:
- simple, clear words
- human-centered phrasing

Avoid:
- heavy jargon
- overly corporate tone

---

## Tone behavior by slide type

Solution:
- explain how it helps people

Value:
- focus on real-world impact

Proof:
- keep data but explain meaning

---

## Sentence style

- natural sentences
- easy to read
- moderate length

---

## Examples

Bad:
"Enable omnichannel orchestration capabilities"

Good:
"Connect every channel so customers experience one seamless journey"
